Farnham - Surrey

"Historic market town nestled in the rolling hills of Surrey"

History

Farnham has a magnificent history, with evidence of Stone Age, Roman and Saxon dwellings found here. Many of the Georgian buildings conceal structures of Tudor or even earlier times. Englandâ€™s first Cistercian abbey was built near Farnham. The now ruins of Waverley Abbey are situated in a peaceful loop of the River Wey and still give an impression of the solitude experienced by the monks who founded a monastery here almost 900 years ago.

The Town is crowned by a 12th century castle overlooking the heart of Farnham, built in 1138 AD by Henry de Blois, grandson of William the Conqueror and has been occupied for nearly 900 years, mostly by the powerful Bishops of Winchester. Monarchs from King John to Queen Victoria have all visited.

Events

Farmers Market
(Central Carpark)

Held on the 4th Sunday of every month, this market brings in the great produce throughout the area. With 40 regular stallholders there is always guarenteed to be something you like, from breakfast to lunch or getting ready for dinner.

Summer Food Festival
(Castle Street)

This large event is held in the center of town overlooked by the infamous Castle itself. Over 50 stalls from local areas bring food, clothing and entertainment whilst people flock in the masses just to enjoy the sunshine. There is a large stage with music so there is always something to do or watch.

Beer and Gin Festival
(The Maltings)

Made especially for the fans of a cheeky tipple or 2, this is exactly what is says on the tin. Held in The Malting building, multiple floors and multiple brewers attend to present their years work. Many stalls offer tasting, bottles and even tours through their distillery or brewery.
